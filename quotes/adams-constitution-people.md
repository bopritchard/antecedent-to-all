# Quotes: John Adams on Constitution and Moral/Religious People

## The Famous Quote

### Variant 1 (Most Common)
> "Our Constitution was made only for a moral and religious people. It is wholly inadequate to the government of any other."

**Source:** John Adams, letter to the Officers of the First Brigade of the Third Division of the Militia of Massachusetts, October 11, 1798

**Context:** Adams writing about the need for virtue in a republic.

**Full Context:**
> "We have no government armed with power capable of contending with human passions unbridled by morality and religion. Avarice, ambition, revenge, or gallantry, would break the strongest cords of our Constitution as a whale goes through a net. Our Constitution was made only for a moral and religious people. It is wholly inadequate to the government of any other."

---

### Variant 2 (Similar)
> "The Constitution was made only for a moral and religious people. It is wholly inadequate to the government of any other."

**Source:** Same letter, sometimes quoted slightly differently.

## Other Relevant Adams Quotes

### On Religion and Government
> "Religion and virtue are the only foundations, not only of republicanism and of all free government, but of social felicity under all governments and in all the combinations of human society."

**Source:** John Adams, letter to Zabdiel Adams, June 21, 1776

---

### On Virtue in Republics
> "Public virtue cannot exist in a nation without private, and public virtue is the only foundation of republics."

**Source:** John Adams, letter to Mercy Warren, April 16, 1776

---

## How to Use in Article

**Key Points:**
- This is Adams's **opinion**, not constitutional canon
- But it is **instructive** - shows founding-era understanding
- Connects to natural law/moral foundation that precedes government
- Shows Constitution assumes virtue, doesn't manufacture it
- Government is "referee, not redeemer" - preserves conditions, doesn't create virtue

**Connection to Article:**
- Constitution assumes moral/religious people
- But doesn't mean state enforces religious commands
- Different from using state to enforce biblical commands
- Shows distinction between moral foundation and state enforcement
