# Research: New Testament Passages on Caring for Poor and Outcast

## Key Passages

### Matthew 25:31-46 (The Sheep and the Goats)
> "For I was hungry and you gave me food, I was thirsty and you gave me drink, I was a stranger and you welcomed me, I was naked and you clothed me, I was sick and you visited me, I was in prison and you came to me... Truly, I say to you, as you did it to one of the least of these my brothers, you did it to me."

**Context:** Jesus describing final judgment, what separates sheep from goats.

**How it's used:** Cited to demand state action for the poor, immigrants, prisoners.

**Contextual issues:**
- Addressed to **disciples** (Jesus's "brothers")
- Commands to **individuals/church**, not the state
- "Least of these my brothers" - fellow believers, not all humanity
- Personal responsibility, not government mandate

---

### James 1:27
> "Religion that is pure and undefiled before God the Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their affliction, and to keep oneself unstained from the world."

**Context:** James describing true religion.

**How it's used:** Argued to require state welfare programs.

**Contextual issues:**
- Addressed to **believers** ("religion that is pure")
- Personal/ecclesial responsibility
- Not a command to the state

---

### James 2:14-17
> "What good is it, my brothers, if someone says he has faith but does not have works? Can that faith save him? If a brother or sister is poorly clothed and lacking in daily food, and one of you says to them, 'Go in peace, be warmed and filled,' without giving them the things needed for the body, what good is that? So also faith by itself, if it does not have works, is dead."

**Context:** James on faith and works.

**How it's used:** Cited to argue for state welfare.

**Contextual issues:**
- Addressed to **believers** ("my brothers")
- Personal responsibility within the church
- Not a state mandate

---

### 1 John 3:17-18
> "But if anyone has the world's goods and sees his brother in need, yet closes his heart against him, how does God's love abide in him? Little children, let us not love in word or talk but in deed and in truth."

**Context:** John on love in action.

**How it's used:** Argued to require government action.

**Contextual issues:**
- Addressed to **believers** ("brother," "little children")
- Personal responsibility
- Not a state command

---

### Galatians 6:10
> "So then, as we have opportunity, let us do good to everyone, and especially to those who are of the household of faith."

**Context:** Paul on doing good.

**How it's used:** Cited to demand state action for all.

**Contextual issues:**
- Addressed to **believers** ("us")
- Personal responsibility
- "Especially to those of the household of faith" - priority to believers
- Not a state mandate

---

### Luke 10:25-37 (The Good Samaritan)
> "But a Samaritan, as he journeyed, came to where he was, and when he saw him, he had compassion. He went to him and bound up his wounds, pouring on oil and wine. Then he set him on his own animal and brought him to an inn and took care of him."

**Context:** Jesus answering "Who is my neighbor?"

**How it's used:** Argued to require state action for all in need.

**Contextual issues:**
- Parable about **personal** responsibility
- Individual action, not government mandate
- Addressed to individual, not the state

---

## Pattern: All Addressed to Believers/Church

### Common Elements
- All addressed to **believers** or the **church**
- Personal/ecclesial responsibility
- Not commands to the **state**
- Not mandates to force others to comply

### The Key Question
- How did these become commands to the state?
- Where is the biblical mandate to offload these onto government?
- Why should non-kingdom people be compelled to fund and participate?
