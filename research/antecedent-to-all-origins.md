# Research: "Antecedent to All" - Origins and Constitutional Meaning

## Possible Meanings in Constitutional Context

Given the departmentalist/originalist framework, "antecedent to all" likely refers to:

### 1. The Constitution Itself as Antecedent
- The text and original intent as antecedent to all judicial interpretation
- The original compact as antecedent to all modern doctrine
- What the document *actually says* and what framers *intended* as antecedent to what courts have *constructed*

### 2. Popular Sovereignty as Antecedent
- The people's authority as antecedent to judicial power
- Popular sovereignty as antecedent to judicial construction
- The original compact between people and government as antecedent to judicial supremacy

### 3. Original Authority as Antecedent
- Founding-era understanding as antecedent to post-constitutional interpretations
- The framers' vision as antecedent to judicial oligarchy
- Text and historical context as antecedent to *stare decisis*

## Potential Sources to Investigate

### Jeffersonian References
- Jefferson's warnings about judicial oligarchy
- Jefferson's departmentalist view of constitutional interpretation
- Jefferson on popular sovereignty vs. judicial power

### Founding-Era Language
- "Antecedent" in 18th century constitutional discourse
- References to what comes "before" or "prior to" government
- Language about original compact or original authority

### Constitutional Text
- Preamble: "We the People" as antecedent authority
- Article VI: Constitution as "supreme Law" - but who interprets?
- Original distribution of powers before Marbury
