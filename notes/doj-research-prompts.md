# DOJ Creation Research Prompts

These prompts can be used with Cursor to explore the historical context and constitutional basis of the Department of Justice.

---

## 1. Historical Context Summary

**Prompt:**

> Summarize the historical reasons for creating the U.S. Department of Justice in 1870, including its legal and political backdrop.

**Use this to fill:** `research/doj-creation-1870.md` section on "Reasons for Creating the DOJ in 1870"

**Context for Cursor:**
- Focus on original authority and historical context
- Distinguish between legal and political reasons
- Consider Reconstruction era context

## 2. Attorney General Evolution

**Prompt:**

> Explain how the role of the Attorney General evolved from 1789 to the creation of the DOJ in 1870.

**Use this to fill:** `research/doj-creation-1870.md` section on "Evolution of the Attorney General (1789-1870)"

**Context for Cursor:**
- Start with Judiciary Act of 1789
- Trace changes over 81 years
- Explain why evolution necessitated full department

## 3. Constitutional Basis

**Prompt:**

> List the constitutional basis (if any) for the creation of the DOJ and the Attorney General's role.

**Use this to fill:** `research/doj-creation-1870.md` section on "Constitutional Basis"

**Context for Cursor:**
- Analyze Constitution's text on executive departments
- Consider Article II implications
- Distinguish between constitutional requirement vs. statutory authorization
- Apply originalist perspective - what did framers contemplate?

## How to Use These Prompts

1. Open Cursor Chat
2. Paste `prompts/session-boilerplate.md` first
3. Paste the relevant prompt from above
4. Specify that you're working on `research/doj-creation-1870.md`
5. Ask Cursor to help fill in the relevant section
6. Emphasize originalist/textualist approach - what does Constitution actually say?

## Additional Questions to Explore

- Was DOJ creation a response to specific constitutional problems?
- How does DOJ's expansion relate to judicial supremacy critique?
- Does DOJ's current scope exceed original constitutional understanding?
- What would framers have thought about a centralized Justice Department?
- How does DOJ relate to departmentalist theory of constitutional interpretation?

## Connection to Core Themes

### Original Authority
- What did Constitution actually authorize regarding executive departments?
- Was DOJ contemplated by framers, or is it post-constitutional construction?

### Executive Power
- Does DOJ represent legitimate executive power or expansion beyond original intent?
- How does DOJ's evolution relate to concerns about executive overreach?

### Constitutional Drift
- Does DOJ's current form represent "constitutional amnesia"?
- Has it maintained original structure while abandoning original purpose?

### Departmentalism
- How does DOJ's role in constitutional interpretation relate to departmentalist theory?
- Does DOJ support or undermine departmental interpretation?
