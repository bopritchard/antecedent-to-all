## How to Use These Prompts

1. Open Cursor Chat
2. Paste `prompts/session-boilerplate.md` first
3. Paste the relevant prompt from above
4. Specify that you're working on `research/doj-independence-vs-executive-authority.md`
5. Emphasize originalist/textualist approach
6. Ask Cursor to analyze from constitutional text and original intent, not modern practice

## Key Questions to Explore

- What does Article II actually say about executive control?
- Did framers contemplate independent executive departments?
- Is unitary executive required by Constitution or just a theory?
- How does DOJ independence relate to democratic accountability?
- What would original understanding say about this tension?

## Connection to Core Themes

### Original Authority
- Constitution's text on executive power
- Original intent regarding executive control
- What framers actually contemplated

### Unitary Executive
- Constitutional basis for unitary executive
- How DOJ independence conflicts with this principle
- Originalist analysis of executive control

### Departmentalism
- How DOJ independence relates to departmentalist interpretation
- Whether independence supports or undermines departmentalist theory

### Constitutional Drift
- Whether DOJ independence represents drift from original understanding
- How practice has diverged from constitutional text

### Democratic Accountability
- How independence affects popular sovereignty
- Whether independence undermines electoral accountability
- Constitutional requirements for accountability
